I learned the most from problem set #3 where the energy balance equation and 1D heat conduction were introduced. With an added layer of an insulating layer to systems, I was able to practice correctly identifying boundary conditions, where dT/dx at certain layers equal 0 due to constant temperature. I also deepened my understanding of nonlinear vs. linear systems when solving conduction problems. Previously, I always assumed the system is linear, but after volumetric generated heat was introduced, I finally grasped the conservation of energy in terms of heat transfer. I learned that when heat is generated within the system, it is no longer linear because heat is being added with respect to time. 
In addition to all the conceptual aspects, I also learned how to approach the problem through units. I was reminded of the importance of units because they served as hints in some parts that lead to the final answer. For example, in question 3, I was initially confused about what R” meant, but knowing the relationship between thermal resistance and heat rate, I was able to solve for its unit, giving a hint on how to find maximum allowable temperature. 
Overall, this homework assignment reminded me of fundamental skills and concepts that eventually help me understand new, harder concepts better.
